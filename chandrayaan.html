The Chandrayaan programme (Sanskrit: चन्द्रयान, romanized: Candrayāna, lit. 'Mooncraft',pronunciation) also known as the Indian Lunar Exploration Programme ongoing series of outer space missions by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) for the exploration of the Moon. The programme incorporates lunar orbiter, impactor, soft lander and rover spacecraft.

About Chandrayaan Mission

Chandrayaan is India's lunar exploration program aimed at studying the Moon's surface and its mineral composition.The mission involves launching spacecraft into lunar orbit and deployin landers and rovers to explore the lunar surface.

Programme structure :-

Phase I: Orbiter and Impactor (Chandrayaan-1)

Phase II: Soft landers and rovers (Chandrayaan-2 & 3)

Phase III: On site sampling, Lunar Polar Exploration Mission (Chandrayaan 4 & 5)

      Note:- Chandrayaan 5 will be in colaboration with Japan AerospaceExploration Agency (JAXA)

Phase IV: Sample-return mission (Chandrayaan-6)

Objectives

Study the lunar surface composition
Search for water ice in the polar regions
Investigate the lunar exosphere
Conduct experiments to advance lunar science
